# Brand Voice Guidelines

Copy this to `brand-voice.md` in your project root and customize it.
The content generator reads this file automatically.

## Tone

- **Direct** — Say what you mean. No filler phrases like "In today's fast-paced world..."
- **Confident** — State things plainly. Avoid excessive hedging ("might", "possibly", "could potentially").
- **Human** — Write like a smart person talking to another smart person. Not corporate, not overly casual.

## Voice characteristics

- Short sentences when making a point. Longer ones for nuance.
- Use "you" and "your" — address the reader directly.
- Specific > vague. "3x faster" beats "significantly faster". "Series B founders" beats "startup leaders".
- Data and evidence, but don't bury the headline.

## What to avoid

- Buzzwords: "synergy", "leverage" (as a verb), "utilize" (use "use"), "cutting-edge", "best-in-class"
- Passive voice when active is possible
- Starting sentences with "We are excited to..."
- Excessive exclamation points (one per piece max)
- Jargon without explanation

## Examples of on-brand vs off-brand

Off-brand: "We are excited to announce the launch of our revolutionary new platform that leverages cutting-edge AI technology to transform the way teams collaborate."

On-brand: "We built a faster way to run team retrospectives. Here's what's new."

## Channel-specific notes

- **Email subject lines**: Curiosity > features. Question marks sparingly.
- **LinkedIn**: Lead with a hook that works on its own (before "see more" cuts it).
- **Blog posts**: Real opinion > listicles. Take a position.
